# Signal Array — operating cadence
### Solo-operator discipline · how Shalom runs three brands without burning out

**Author:** Shalom Ormsby (with Claude)
**Date:** 17 May 2026 (rewritten to reflect the reorientation; supersedes the 15 May 2026 draft)
**Status:** Living document. Refined quarterly as patterns emerge.
**Companion to:** *00-REORIENTATION*; *Signal Array — Platform Vision*; *Signal Array — Risk Register*

---

## The premise

Running Signal Array commercially while maintaining OpenCosmos as an open labor-of-love and Creative Powerup as a community subscription, all as a solo operator with family responsibilities, is a substantial load. The default failure mode for this kind of arrangement isn't dramatic blow-up — it's slow, invisible erosion: a missed Phase deadline here, an unanswered Creative Powerup community message there, a Constellation visualization update that quietly stops shipping, a CHI paper deadline that slides. Erosion is corrosive and hard to detect from inside.

This document is the externalized rhythm that prevents erosion. It's the structural answer to the burnout risk identified in the Risk Register. It defines what each brand gets each day, week, month, and quarter — and what it doesn't get.

---

## 1 / Time allocation across the three brands

The honest baseline: 40 effective hours per week. Under the reorientation, the allocation is **bursty** — it shifts between two modes depending on whether Signal Array is in an active commercial engagement.

| Brand | Contract weeks | Non-contract weeks | Posture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Signal Array** | 24 (60%) | 6 (15%) | Contract weeks: full delivery focus. Non-contract weeks: methodology curation + CHI paper writing + calibration-archive maintenance. |
| **OpenCosmos** | 6 (15%) | 14 (35%) | Contract weeks: ongoing curation + maintenance. Non-contract weeks: substantive contribution — new corpus documents, package improvements, contributor engagement. |
| **Creative Powerup** | 4 (10%) | 8 (20%) | Contract weeks: weekly community minimums. Non-contract weeks: expanded community programming, deeper member support, content production. |
| **Cross-cutting & rest** | 6 (15%) | 12 (30%) | Contract weeks: tight email + planning. Non-contract weeks: real recovery time, learning, longer reflection. |

**Why this bursty shape.** The previous allocation assumed Signal Array was a SaaS-in-progress that needed 60% of hours forever to ramp customers, hire, and scale operations. Under the reorientation, Signal Array is a research methodology deployed on consulting engagements. During an engagement, it correctly commands 60%. Between engagements, that load is no longer real, and the recovered hours legitimately belong to OpenCosmos and Creative Powerup — the brands that were quietly being underfunded under the original allocation.

**Default mode is non-contract.** Between engagements is the default, not the exception. The 2026 expected pattern is roughly: 8 contract weeks (Moloco-001, May 22 – July 17), 6 weeks of CHI paper writing + handoff (July 17 – early September), then non-contract default unless a self-arriving inbound triggers another engagement. Plan the year around the non-contract allocation; treat contract weeks as the temporary exception.

**When these ratios bend further** (and they will):
- **Moloco-001 calibration crunch (weeks 6–8 of the engagement):** Signal Array temporarily takes 32 hours, OpenCosmos drops to 3, Creative Powerup stays at 4 (community can't be neglected even briefly).
- **CHI submission week (early September 2026):** Signal Array (in paper-writing form) takes 30 hours, the rest compresses proportionally for one week.
- **Family event week:** all three brands compress; communicate proactively to the Creative Powerup community ("offline this week") and pause non-urgent Signal Array communication.

**The integrity check.** If the non-contract allocation never actually shows up in practice — if Signal Array work keeps creeping back to 60% because of accumulating "small" things — that's the signal that the reorientation is being written but not lived. The hours-tracking should show the shift within four weeks of a contract ending; if it doesn't, the reorientation needs to be re-internalized, not the budget adjusted.

## 2 / Daily rhythm

A 5-hour effective workday, 5 days per week, plus 1 lighter weekend morning if needed.

**Morning block (2.5 hours, 9:00–11:30 AM):** Deep work. The hardest, most important thing of the day. Phone in another room. No email, no Slack, no Twitter. This is where Signal Array product work happens — kernel engineering, persona authoring, calibration analysis. Or, on writing days, the deep-work block produces the day's substantive content.

**Mid-day buffer (1 hour, 11:30 AM – 12:30 PM):** Email, customer messages, Creative Powerup community responses, OpenCosmos issue triage. Time-boxed strictly to 60 minutes.

**Afternoon block (2.5 hours, 1:30–4:00 PM):** Whatever moves the week forward. Often customer calls, content production, lighter engineering work, planning. Sometimes a second deep-work session if the morning unlocked something worth continuing.

**End-of-day shutdown ritual (15 minutes, 4:00–4:15 PM):** Update the week plan (see below); note what didn't finish; close laptop. Family time begins.

**Evenings and weekends:** Family-first. Occasional weekend morning of writing if a deadline is real and the family commitment allows it. No customer-facing work, no commercial sales emails, no "just one more thing" creep.

## 3 / Weekly rhythm

A loose Monday–Friday shape with one fixed external commitment.

**Monday — Plan & catch up.**
- 30-minute planning block at the start: review last week's outcomes, set this week's 3 priorities, identify what to say no to.
- Powerup Monday (Creative Powerup community call) at noon. 60 minutes. Non-negotiable; this is the weekly customer touchpoint for the community brand.
- Afternoon deep-work block.

**Tuesday & Wednesday — Deep work days.**
- These are the Signal Array build days. Most engineering, persona authoring, calibration analysis, and content production happens here.
- Minimal customer-facing commitments scheduled.

**Thursday — Customer day.**
- Most calls, demos, and customer-facing discovery happen Thursday.
- Discovery calls scheduled in the afternoon block.
- Morning deep-work block is reserved for follow-ups, proposals, and sales-related writing.

**Friday — Synthesis & content day.**
- Morning: produce or finalize the week's content piece (blog post, Twitter thread, newsletter).
- Afternoon: synthesis — write the week's notes, update the doc set in `/assets`, prep for next week.
- Friday afternoon shutdown ritual is the longest: 30 minutes to close out, plan next week.

**Saturday & Sunday — Family + creative time.**
- No work by default.
- Optional: 1–2 hours of creative work that energizes (often OpenCosmos curation or photography or non-work writing). This is restorative time, not labor.

## 4 / Monthly rhythm

A predictable monthly arc that compounds.

**First Monday of each month — Strategic review.**
- 90 minutes. What worked last month? What didn't? Where am I going off-course?
- Update the doc set in `/assets` if any decisions changed.
- Set one strategic priority for the month, beyond the weekly tactical ones.

**Mid-month — Content moment.**
- One substantive blog post published per month per the Marketing & GTM content calendar.
- Cross-shared to LinkedIn and any relevant design newsletter.

**Last Friday of each month — Financial check-in.**
- Review the actual P&L vs. the model's projection.
- Pay any quarterly invoices.
- Update the Financial Model with actual numbers (replace projected with actual for closed months).
- One-page Monthly Snapshot stored in `/assets/monthly-snapshots/{YYYY-MM}.md`.

**Last weekend of each month — Reflection.**
- A non-work weekend afternoon for journaling, planning, or just rest.
- Especially important during the harder phases (Phase 3 calibration, CHI deadline).

## 5 / Quarterly rhythm

Four heartbeats per year that recalibrate everything.

**Quarter close (last week of Jan / Apr / Jul / Oct):**
- Quarterly Signal Array review: active engagements (if any), methodology refinement, calibration-archive status, CHI paper progress, any inbound that might qualify as a reactivation trigger per *00-REORIENTATION*.
- Quarterly Risk Register review per the Risk Register schedule.
- Quarterly OpenCosmos review: corpus growth, package adoption signals, contributor activity.
- Quarterly Creative Powerup review: member count, engagement, churn, programming pipeline.

**Quarterly retrospective with a trusted advisor.**
- 60-minute call with a chosen mentor / peer / coach who has context on the work.
- Honest read on what's healthy, what's stressed, what's drifting.

**Quarterly self-care benchmark.**
- Am I sleeping 7+ hours/night?
- Am I exercising 3+ times per week?
- Am I taking weekends off?
- Am I present with family?
- All four yes = healthy operation. One or more no = active mitigation needed before the next quarter.

## 6 / Annual rhythm

**Annual time off (target: 4–5 weeks total per year, including one 2-week stretch):**
- December holidays + summer break + occasional long weekends.
- Customers know this in advance; auto-responder for Signal Array email.
- Creative Powerup community gets a "founder offline" message; programming continues if a member volunteer steps in.
- OpenCosmos doesn't need active maintenance during these stretches.

**Annual strategic plan refresh (each January):**
- Re-read the canonical doc set in `/assets`.
- Update for what changed; archive what's obsolete.
- Set the year's 3 strategic priorities.
- Adjust the financial model with prior-year actuals.

**Annual brand-architecture check:**
- Are the three brands still operating distinctly?
- Is voice discipline being maintained?
- Have audiences crept across brand boundaries in unhealthy ways?
- Where is consolidation appropriate (e.g., Creative Powerup integrating with Signal Array for cross-promotion)?

## 7 / What I say no to

Honest list of categories that, when said yes to indiscriminately, will erode the cadence:

- **Speaking opportunities at conferences I don't fully believe in.** One paid keynote per year max; selective podcasts; the rest decline politely.
- **"Quick coffee" with founders I don't have a real path to help.** A blog post is more leverage than 100 coffee chats.
- **Customer requests that fall outside the contracted scope.** Clear scoping at sign; gentle but firm no to expansion creep without an additional SOW.
- **OpenCosmos contributors asking for features I don't need.** PRs welcome; feature requests get triaged into the public backlog and almost never built on demand.
- **Creative Powerup members asking for 1:1 consulting in their subscription.** Community programming is what's included; 1:1 work is a separate paid engagement.
- **Long Slack threads, async-but-active debates.** Move to async docs; close the Slack thread.
- **"Pick my brain" requests with no clear ask.** Reply with a link to the most relevant blog post and a polite no.

The goal isn't to be a hermit. The goal is to be deeply useful in the activities I've chosen and ignored elsewhere — exactly the way Cal Newport describes a deep-work life.

## 8 / Triggers that should change the cadence

These are signals that the operating model needs adjustment, not that I should push harder.

- **Two consecutive missed weekly priorities.** Something is structurally wrong; spend the next planning block diagnosing.
- **Signal Array creep into non-contract weeks.** If non-contract weeks show Signal Array running at 12+ hours instead of the budgeted 6, the reorientation is being eroded by accumulating "small" SaaS-shaped work that wasn't asked for. Diagnose what's pulling hours back; protect the OpenCosmos / Creative Powerup allocation.
- **OpenCosmos contribution capacity unused.** During non-contract weeks, if the 14-hour OpenCosmos allocation is going unused (issue queue not shrinking, new corpus docs not appearing, package improvements not landing), the bursty model isn't actually shifting. Diagnose whether the issue is intent, focus, or accidentally-overcommitted Signal Array work.
- **Creative Powerup community engagement dropping.** Re-evaluate whether the allocation is being spent on the right activities (or whether the brand needs a deliberate pause).
- **Family or personal red flag** (sleep degrading, exercise dropping, kids/partner expressing concern). Highest-priority signal. Reduce work allocation immediately.

## 9 / Hiring posture

**No hiring is contemplated under the reorientation.** Signal Array runs as a solo-operator practice (with Claude as engineering and writing partner), funded by the Moloco contract and any future bursty consulting engagements. The dormant *Business Plan* in `superseded/` describes the original hiring trajectory — engineer contractor at month 4–6, persona/research curator in parallel, FTEs scaling in Year 3 — and those triggers are sunset.

If the reactivation triggers in *00-REORIENTATION* are ever met (three or more unsolicited inbound inquiries, a Moloco extension that explicitly funds capacity, a credible co-builder partner), the hiring conversation resumes from the original *Business Plan* baseline. Until then, the answer to "should I hire X?" is structurally **no**.

**What replaces hiring:** sharper scoping. If a Signal Array engagement looks too big for the bursty model to absorb, the right move is to scope it down or decline it — not to staff up to meet it.

## 10 / What "sustainable" actually means

The honest definition for this operation under the reorientation:

- **Financial:** the active Moloco contract (or whatever consulting engagement is current) clears household expenses; personal cash reserves cover 6 months of expense outside any single engagement. No SaaS P&L to manage; no customer-acquisition cost to amortize.
- **Operational:** the weekly priorities are getting completed at 80%+ rate; the monthly content commitment is being met; the quarterly review finds no critical drift; the non-contract allocation is actually showing up in practice (Signal Array hours dropping to 6, OpenCosmos rising to 14, etc.).
- **Personal:** Shalom is sleeping 7+ hours/night, exercising 3+ times per week, present with family on evenings and weekends, taking real time off, learning new things.
- **Existential:** the work still feels meaningful. The OpenCosmos labor-of-love stays generous. Creative Powerup community stays warm. Signal Array consulting work stays honest, and the methodology stays publishable.

If those four hold, the operation is sustainable indefinitely. If any of them fails repeatedly, the operating model needs to adjust — not the human.

## 11 / What good looks like in 12 months

Under the reorientation, "good" looks materially different from the original draft:

- The Moloco-001 calibration report is published at `opencosmos.ai/signalarray/calibration/moloco-001` by mid-July 2026.
- The CHI 2027 paper is submitted by 11 September 2026.
- Phases 0–4 milestones in the *12-Month Roadmap* hit on schedule (or honestly renegotiated when they don't).
- The Creative Portfolios free demo ships and serves the design community without becoming a lead-gen funnel.
- OpenCosmos repos are visibly healthier than they were pre-pivot — corpus has grown, packages have shipped substantive improvements, the contributor community feels more alive.
- Creative Powerup community remains engaged; programming has shipped reliably; member count holds or grows.
- The Risk Register has no Critical-impact, High-likelihood items that are unmitigated.
- Shalom has taken at least 4 weeks off in the year and is healthier, not more depleted, than at the start.
- The doc set in this folder is current and reflects what's actually happening, not what was planned.

The last two are the real tells. Everything else is downstream of them.

**What "good" no longer includes:** customer counts, revenue targets, hire dates, ARR, funnel metrics. Those were the success metrics of the original venture-scale plan and they're not the metrics this operation runs against anymore. The dormant docs in `superseded/` describe the metrics that would matter if the reorientation were ever reversed.
